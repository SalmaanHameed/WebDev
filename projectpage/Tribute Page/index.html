Richard Phillips Feynman



Richard Feynman was a Nobel Prize-winning American physicist, particularly known for his contributions to quantum physics, quantum electrodynamics and particle physics, as well as quantum computing and nanotechnology. During his lifetime, he was one of the best-known scientists in the world, and was a great popularizer of physics through his books and lectures.He used to play the bongos at clubs and drink heavily.He was also regarded as something of an eccentric and free spirit.


Awards and Achievements


  • Albert Einstein Award (1954)

  • E. O. Lawrence Award (1962)

  • Nobel Prize in Physics (1965)

  • Foreign Member of the Royal Society (1965)

  • Oersted Medal (1972)

  • National Medal of Science (1979)


Books Published


Feynman published many books during his lifetime.Some of the most famous books are:



  • “Surely You’re Joking, Mr Feynman!” Adventures Of A Curious Character

  • The Pleasure Of Finding Things Out

  • The Character Of Physical Law

  • The Feynman Lectures on Physics, Vol. I

  • The Feynman Lectures on Physics, Vol. II

  • The Feynman Lectures on Physics, Vol. III
